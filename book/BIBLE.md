# Book Bible — El niño que vino del mar / ninio-mar

## Canon overrides

These rules override older drafts and any memory-based completion:

- Replace pear / Grandmother Pear with **El manzano de invierno**.
- Use **la enfermedad del injerto**, not blight.
- Do **not** use Pacific as origin for El niño que vino del mar.
- Keep **vino del mar** broad, genealogical, and multigenerational.
- Use Montreal as: **“Montreal, donde el río finge memoria de mar.”**
- Mention San Lorenzo only as river-memory of sea.
- Let the phonetic-symbolic field operate silently.
- Do not cluster: abuela + bedtime story + niño del mar + Aurora + luz + enfermedad + omisión.
- Do not cluster: legal documents + medication + omission + altered states + domestic utensils.
- Remove all “puerta falsa” imagery.
- When a symbol begins pointing to one living person, split it, delay it, or move it into object/weather.

## Functional frame

The material belongs to different narrative organs:

- memoir
- family myth
- educational repair manual
- allegory
- courtroom shadow
- love story
- neurodivergent survival guide
- transgenerational archive

Do not force these into simple chronology.

## Ethical frame

The book is autobiographical in origin but not documentary in method. Some figures are composites. Some scenes are remembered, some inherited, some overheard, some allegorical, some quarantined, and some deliberately displaced to protect living people.

The emotional architecture may be true while the legal surface is not offered as evidence.

## Narrative current model

1. Autobiographical current — what happened, or what can be safely approached.
2. Allegorical/educational current — maintenance, trees, latch, ledger.
3. Fragment current — overheard speech, family secrets, damaged memories.
4. Retrospective current — Leuchtturm, house, trees, final reclassification.

## Humor rule

Use humor when the system is pretending to be dignified. Avoid humor when a child, private wound, or recognizable person is exposed without protection.

## Anti-verdict rule

Do not write to expose people. Write to expose mechanisms.

The people may still feel exposed. The defense is structural accuracy, composite protection, and refusal to make anyone only villain, only victim, only axe, or only tree.
